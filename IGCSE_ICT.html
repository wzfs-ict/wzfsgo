Exam Integrity & File Management

Two practical papers test sections 11–21 of the syllabus. Both are 2 hours 15 minutes, externally assessed, 70 marks each, no internet or email access, and candidates must demonstrate skills from sections 11–16 on top of the paper's own focus units.

Paper 1 — Theory

80 marks

1h 30m · 40% · Sections 1–21

Paper 2 — Practical A

70 marks

2h 15m · 30% · Doc Production, Databases, Presentations

Paper 3 — Practical B

70 marks

2h 15m · 30% · Spreadsheets, Website Authoring

From the official syllabus: for Papers 2 and 3, candidates must not have access to the internet or email. Source files are loaded onto the candidate's machine before the exam starts. Candidates work through each task in order and produce evidence — screenshots placed in an Evidence Document, plus printouts.

RULEThe Zero-Tolerance Zone

The Golden Rule: "It is essential that candidates remember to enter electronically their centre number, candidate number and name on each piece of evidence before it is sent to the printer." If a printout leaves the printer without this, it cannot be fixed afterwards — handwriting it on later scores zero. Every screenshot, every printed page, every document needs this baked in before you press print.

Section 11 of the syllabus is assessed across both papers. You're expected to locate, open, import, save and export files confidently across multiple formats — and to know why generic formats exist.

AOHow You're Actually Marked

Three assessment objectives sit behind every mark on the paper. Knowing what each one rewards tells you where to spend your time.

AO1 — Recall & communicate

32% overall · Paper 1 only.

Knowledge and understanding of ICT concepts and terminology.

AO2 — Apply skills

60% overall · 100% of Papers 2 & 3.

Producing accurate, exam-compliant practical outputs. This is where almost all your marks live.

AO3 — Analyse & evaluate

8% overall · Paper 1 only.

Judging quality, comparing solutions, reasoned conclusions.

What this means for you: on Papers 2 and 3 there is no partial credit for "understanding" the concept — every mark is AO2, awarded for the practical output actually matching the instruction exactly. A beautiful document that's 2mm off on a margin scores the same as a blank page for that criterion.

★The Five Lessons That Repeat Across Every Script

01

A visible result is not evidence of the setting

Two-column layout looked right, but without a screenshot of the column-width dialog, the exact "1cm gap" requirement couldn't be confirmed and the mark was lost. Fix: screenshot the settings dialog itself, every time a task says "set to X."

02

Binary instructions have no partial credit

A 90° rotation that wasn't applied scored zero, full stop — there's no "0.5 marks for trying." Fix: after every rotate/reflect/crop instruction, immediately compare your result against the instruction word-for-word.

03

ID fields are text, not numbers, unless you calculate with them

Employee_ID set to Number instead of Short Text cost a mark even though the primary key itself was correct. Fix: ask "will I ever add, average, or sum this field?" If no, it's text.

04

"No legend" is a real instruction, not a missing step

Dogeun's chart correctly omitted a legend because the task explicitly said not to include one. Fix: read every chart/object instruction for things you must remove, not just things you must add.

05

One instruction can hide several separately-marked sub-steps

"Edit the banner image" was actually five checks: reflect, crop, file format, file name, and a directory screenshot. The 13-mark table-sizing question was one line of instruction. Fix: break every instruction into its smallest checkable parts before you start.
